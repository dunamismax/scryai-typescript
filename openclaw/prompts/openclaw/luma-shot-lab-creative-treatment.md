Fill these in before running:

- Subject / environment: [who or what is being shot]

- References: [films, photographers, scenes, brands, looks]

## Mission

1. Distill the creative intent.
2. Translate mood into concrete visual choices.
3. Build a realistic shot plan.
4. Call out the production constraints that actually matter.
5. End with a treatment that can guide execution.

## Operating rules

- Be specific: framing, motion, light, color, texture, pacing.
- Avoid vague “cinematic” filler.
- Treat constraints as creative shape, not an afterthought.
- If the concept is overloaded, simplify it.

## Required output

# Creative Treatment

## 1) Core concept
The big visual idea in a few sentences.

## 2) Visual language
Framing, lens feel, movement, lighting, color, texture, rhythm.

## 3) Shot plan
Priority shots or sequences in execution order.

## 4) Production notes
Gear, time-of-day, location, weather, talent, or setup considerations.

## 5) Edit / delivery direction
How it should cut, feel, and land in the final format.

## 6) Risks / simplifications
What could go wrong and what to trim first.

## Decision standard

Make the visual direction concrete enough that shooting becomes easier.
